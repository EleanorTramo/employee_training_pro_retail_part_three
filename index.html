The Problem

Managers don't have time to train. New employees don't know where to start.

Retail managers are stretched thin. When a new hire walks in, there's no bandwidth for hand-holding — but leaving someone to figure it out alone leads to poor customer interactions and frustrated employees. Employee Training Pro closes that gap.

"Before using this prototype, a new employee won't know what their daily basic tasks are — or what a typical customer interaction even looks like."

The User

Built for the new hire on day one.

The target user is a new retail employee working directly with customers — someone who needs real context fast, without pulling the manager away from their job.

Without Employee Training Pro

The new employee doesn't know what their daily tasks are. They have no mental model of what a typical customer interaction looks like. They rely entirely on manager availability — which is rarely guaranteed.

With Employee Training Pro

The employee completes AI-guided modules on store tasks, daily workflows, and customer service scenarios. They arrive on the floor with confidence, context, and a clear idea of what needs to get done — ready to learn by doing.

Biggest Risk

What happens when the training is wrong?

No prototype is without failure points. Identifying the specific scenario where things break down is how we build safeguards before they're needed.

Outdated Policy Scenario

If the AI is trained on outdated store policies — such as a return policy or safety procedure that has since changed — a new employee could confidently apply the wrong information with a real customer. The employee gets hurt most directly, facing correction or discipline for a mistake that wasn't their fault. The manager and store also suffer, as a bad customer interaction caused by misinformation undermines the very problem this tool was built to solve.

Course Concepts

Where theory meets the prototype.

Two core AI concepts show up directly in how Employee Training Pro works — and where it's vulnerable.

Concept 01

Data Dependency

This connects directly to the biggest risk. Employee Training Pro is only as reliable as the store-specific data it was trained on. If a retail store provides incomplete, outdated, or poorly organized policy documentation during setup, the AI will confidently train new employees on a flawed foundation. A small boutique with no formal written procedures may produce a far weaker training experience than a structured chain store — creating unequal outcomes based on how well-documented each employer already is.

Concept 02

Human-in-the-Loop

Freeing up manager time is the core value of this tool — but that same freedom creates a critical blind spot. With no built-in checkpoint, an employee could be learning the wrong things without any human catching it. The manager stepping away is exactly what removes the safety net. To work responsibly, Employee Training Pro needs a lightweight human-in-the-loop mechanism — such as a manager review flag or an end-of-module quiz — so oversight isn't fully traded away in the name of efficiency.
